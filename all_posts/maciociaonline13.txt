translation of men as most chinese medicine terms have multiple , simultaneous meanings chinese patients will actually use the term men they might say , for example , i sleep badly , i have a bitter phlegm or severe qi stagnation men occurs in the chest or , less frequently , in the epigastrium it does not occur in the lower abdomen the term men first appears in chapter of the su wen where it is described as a feeling of fullness of the chest with anxiety and blurred vision there is another condition characterized by men called rising men qi men qi sheng , a condition that affects newborn babies it is characterized by a feeling of men in the umbilical area , the baby is not crying and has difficulty breathing i men is rich in meaning and , in my experience , it describes not only a
